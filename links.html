 if you are reading the source code of this page you are probably looking for more about what i found. i dont know why i feel the need to keep hiding things in the html. maybe because putting it in the regular text makes it feel real and i dont want it to be real yet. if its in the source code its like a secret. secrets feel safer than facts. i found another name today. in the city records. project hallway wasn't just at central. there were four sites: site a - ramsey county courthouse site b - humboldt high school (demolished 1971) site c - central high school (us) site d - "summit tunnel entrance" summit tunnel. thats summit avenue. the longest continuous stretch of victorian homes in america. the f scott fitzgerald house is on summit. everybody walks down summit and admires the architecture and nobody thinks about what's underneath. everybody in st paul knows about the caves. the wabasha street caves along the river bluffs. the ones they used during prohibition for speakeasies and gangster hideouts. the schmidt brewery caves on west 7th. the ones they used for lagering beer. the old mushroom farms. the land o'lakes cheese caves. everybody knows about those. what nobody talks about is the fort road labyrinth. 30 miles of interconnected sewer tunnels under the city, dug in the st. peter sandstone bedrock more than a hundred years ago. they go under every street at an average depth of 30 feet. some go deeper. the geology here is perfect for tunneling - soft sandstone under a cap of hard platteville limestone. you can dig through it with hand picks. five hundred million years ago this was the floor of a shallow tropical sea. the sandstone is what's left of the sand at the bottom of that sea. the limestone is what's left of the shells of the things that lived in it. the city records reference a tunnel system that connects the caves to all four project hallway sites. not the sewer tunnels. something older. something that was already there when they started digging the sewers. the 1941 city council minutes call them "pre-existing geological formations of uncertain origin." formations. like they were natural. but the fort road labyrinth guys who explored every inch of the sewer system in the 1980s never found anything like what the city describes. because whatever these "formations" are, they go deeper than the sewers. deeper than 30 feet. maybe deeper than anyone has ever dug in this city. whatever is under central high school, it is connected to something bigger. and the seals at the other three sites may also be failing. humboldt was DEMOLISHED in 1971. think about that. they didn't just seal the basement. they tore the whole building down. why? was the seal already failing? is whatever was under humboldt free now? has it been free since 1971? i am sixteen years old. i should be playing quake with ryan and trying to figure out how to ask amanda to the winter formal. instead i am crawling through basements at midnight trying to document something the city of st paul has been hiding for 54 years. somebody help me. please. i dont know what to do anymore. i am a kid. this is too big for me. this is too big for anyone.
