CHS_Alumni_1952 10/28/97 11:22 PM
Young man, I graduated from Central in 1952. My name is Arthur. I live in Roseville now but I grew up on Marshall Avenue, three blocks from the school. I remember the locked door in the basement. We used to dare each other to go down to B2 after basketball practice. The janitor at the time, Mr. Lindqvist, told us to stay away from it. He said "nothing good comes from going deep." I always thought he was just trying to scare us. One time in the winter of 1950 I went down there on a dare from Charlie Olson and I stood at that door for about five minutes. I could hear something through the steel. I told myself it was the boiler. I have been telling myself it was the boiler for forty-five years. After reading your page I am not so sure it was the boiler. Be careful son.

CHS_Alumni_1952 12/15/97 9:30 PM
I went to the school yesterday. I am 63 years old and I drove from Roseville to Lexington Parkway and I walked into that building and I went down to the basement for the first time since 1951. Nobody stopped me. Nobody asked why a 63 year old man was walking into a high school and going to the basement. The door is still there. Same steel door. Same old lock. I put my ear against it like David described on his page. I stood there for ten minutes. I heard it. The breathing. Slow. Rhythmic. One breath every thirty seconds, just like David wrote. God forgive me, I heard it. And underneath the breathing I heard something else. A voice. A boy's voice. Very faint. Very far away. Like it was coming from the bottom of a well. It was saying a word. Over and over. I think the word was "help." I went to the police station on Dale Street and I told them what I heard and they looked at me like I was a crazy old man. They said they had already searched the school and found nothing. I am not crazy. David Mara is down there. That boy is under that school and he is calling for help and nobody is listening.
E. Whitmore III 01/03/98 1:44 AM
I went to the school on 12/29. I brought a flashlight and my grandfather's old key ring that my father gave me after his funeral. One of the keys fit the steel door. It shouldn't have. The lock has been changed at least twice since 1943. But the key fit. Behind the door the stairs went down and at the bottom there was concrete and the concrete was cracked wide open. I could smell it. The same smell my grandfather described in his logs. Copper and something sweet. Sickeningly sweet. Like overripe fruit and meat. I shone my flashlight through the crack and I saw the floor below. It was wet. There were marks in the concrete. Grooves worn into the floor in a circular pattern. And at the edge of the light I saw something move. Something large. I did not stay. I called the St. Paul Police non-emergency line from a payphone on Snelling. They said they would send someone. I do not know if they did. I have not slept more than two hours a night since. I understand now why my grandfather drank. I understand why he died the way he did. David if you are reading this somewhere I am sorry. I should have found your page sooner. I should have warned you about what my family has known about since 1943 and never told anyone because my grandfather was too afraid and too ashamed. He sent people down there. He knew what was happening to them and he didn't stop it and he lived with that for 25 years and it ate him alive. I pray for you.
