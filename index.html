Welcome to my page!!

Hey!! My name is David and im a junior at Central High School in St. Paul MN. This webpage is for my history project for Mr. Aldermans 11th grade U.S. history class where we have to research something local. I picked Central because I figured it would be easy lol. Our school has been around since 1866 which makes it the oldest continuously operating high school in all of Minnesota. Thats pretty cool right??

The building were in right now is at Marshall and Lexington Parkway. It was built in 1912, designed by Clarence H. Johnston Sr. (he also designed a bunch of buildings at the U of M). Its this huge gothic looking building, really beautiful. They renovated it in the late 70s and kind of covered up the original castle looking exterior with cement which sucks but whatever. Theres still a bunch of the original building left if you know where to look. Old stairwells, the original cafeteria behind the new walls, stuff like that.

Anyway the reason I keep working on this is because of what I found in the basement. More on that later.

About This Project:

For this project I went to the St. Paul Public Library on Rice Street, the Minnesota Historical Society on Kellogg Blvd, AND the school archives which are in this storage room in the basement past the boiler room. Ms. Patterson the office manager let me look through the old records down there. Nobody has touched some of those boxes in probably 40 or 50 years.

Most people know about the shooting range in the sub-basement. It was open until 1964 and they cemented over the dirt floor a few years ago. But thats not whats weird. Whats weird is that the building plans I looked at downtown only show two basement levels and the shooting range. But theres a whole other section down there that isnt on any blueprint. The janitor logs from the 1940s talk about a third level below the shooting range. And theres a steel door at the end of the B2 corridor that nobody has a key to.

i went back down there after school on tuesday. i heard it again. it wasnt the boiler. it wasnt the pipes. it was breathing. something down there is breathing. slow. one breath every thirty seconds. i timed it. i put my hand on the door and i could feel it vibrating with each exhale. the metal was warm. Im going to keep researching. Check back for updates.
